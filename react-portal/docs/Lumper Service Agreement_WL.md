Lumper Service Agreement

This Lumper Service Agreement ("Agreement") is made between the parties below and is effective as of the Effective Date.

**Client**

**Provider**

- Carolina Lumper Service, LLC
- Contact: Steve Garay
- Email: info@carolinalumpers.com

1. Scope of Services
   Provider will perform freight unloading ("lumper") services at Client facilities as requested. Provider is a lumper service provider and not a staffing agency. Provider does not lease or supply employees to Client. All crews remain under Provider's management and responsibility. Provider personnel will comply with all Client facility safety policies, OSHA requirements, and site rules at all times.

2. Rates and Pricing Structure
   Pricing is based on actual handling requirements observed at the time of unloading, including pallet height, case count, and unload duration.

2.1 Base Unload Rate

- $300 per container

   Applies to standard unloads of up to 2,000 cases, where:

   - Total pallet height does not exceed 100 inches, and
   - Pallets can be fully built inside the container without ladder-assisted stacking.

2.2 Additional Handling Charges (applied only when required by the load)

   A. High Stack Handling

   - +$150 per container

     Applies when total pallet height exceeds 100 inches, as determined by:

     - Manufacturer pallet load sheets, or
     - Actual pallet builds observed during unloading.

     High Stack Handling requires ladder-assisted stacking and/or multi-stage pallet builds.

   B. High Case Count Handling

   Applies when total case count exceeds 2,000 cases, due to extended handling and repeated pallet builds:

   - 2,001–3,000 cases: +$100
   - 3,001–4,000 cases: +$150

   When both High Stack Handling and High Case Count Handling apply, the total charge is capped at $600 per container for loads up to 4,000 cases.

3. Handling Classification
   Handling classification shall be determined based on actual unload conditions at the dock. Manufacturer pallet load sheets may be used as a reference; however, final classification is based on real-world handling requirements observed during unloading. SKU designation alone does not override physical handling conditions.

4. Scheduling and Access
   Client agrees to:

   - Provide timely access to containers and dock doors
   - Ensure freight is accessible and ready for unloading
   - Provide necessary equipment access (dock plates, pallet jacks, etc.)

   Delays caused by dock congestion, inaccessible freight, or facility issues may extend unload time and impact pricing.

5. Term
   This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated. Either party may terminate with 15 days written notice.

6. Insurance
   Provider maintains general liability and workers' compensation insurance for its personnel and will provide certificates upon request. At a minimum, Provider will maintain: (a) Commercial General Liability insurance with limits of not less than $1,000,000 per occurrence and $2,000,000 aggregate; (b) Workers' Compensation insurance as required by law; (c) Employer's Liability insurance with limits not less than $500,000. All liability policies will name Client as an Additional Insured on a primary and non-contributory basis, with certificates provided before services begin and upon renewal.

7. Independent Contractor
   Provider is an independent contractor and assumes full responsibility for its personnel, including wages, taxes, insurance, and benefits. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employer-employee, joint venture, or staffing relationship between Client and Provider personnel. Provider personnel will not operate or use Client forklifts, pallet jacks, or other material handling equipment without Client's express prior written consent.

8. Worker Conversion Fee
   Client will not directly hire or engage Provider employees or subcontractors during the term of this Agreement and for twelve (12) months after termination. If Client hires or engages any such individual without Provider's prior written consent, Client will pay Provider a conversion fee of $10,000 per individual as liquidated damages.

9. Indemnification
   Each party will indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other party, its affiliates, officers, employees, and agents from and against third-party claims, damages, liabilities, losses, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) to the extent arising out of (i) the negligence or willful misconduct of the indemnifying party or its employees, agents, or subcontractors, or (ii) the breach of this Agreement by the indemnifying party. Additionally, Provider will indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client from and against such claims, damages, liabilities, losses, and expenses arising out of or related to (a) injury to or death of Provider employees, agents, or subcontractors while performing services under this Agreement, or (b) damage to Client equipment, facilities, or cargo resulting from Provider's performance of services, except to the extent caused by Client's negligence, willful misconduct, or failure to provide a reasonably safe work environment.

10. Confidentiality
    Both parties will maintain the confidentiality of proprietary or sensitive information obtained in connection with this Agreement.

11. Dispute Resolution
    The parties will first attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement. If unresolved, the parties will submit the matter to mediation in Mecklenburg County, North Carolina. If mediation is unsuccessful, the dispute will be resolved by binding arbitration under the rules of the American Arbitration Association. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court having jurisdiction.

12. Entire Agreement
    This Agreement is the entire understanding between the parties and supersedes any prior oral or written agreements.
